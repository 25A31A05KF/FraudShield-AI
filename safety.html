Online Safety Tips

Staying safe online is crucial in todayâ€™s digital world. Fraudsters and scammers are constantly finding new ways to trick users. By following simple safety measures, you can protect your personal information, financial data, and digital identity.

1. Protect Your Personal Information

Never share sensitive details such as passwords, OTPs, bank account information, or personal identification numbers over email, social media, or unknown websites.

2. Be Cautious With Links and Attachments

Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources. Scammers often use phishing emails to trick you into sharing information.

3. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

Always use strong, unique passwords for different accounts. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible to add an extra layer of security.

4. Keep Your Devices Secure

Ensure your devices have updated antivirus software and operating system updates. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.

5. Verify Before You Trust

Always verify the source before acting on any message, email, or transaction request. If something seems suspicious, double-check with official channels.

Stay vigilant, stay safe, and protect your digital world!
